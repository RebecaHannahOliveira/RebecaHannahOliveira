# Hi, I'm Rebeca! 👋

I am a biomedical engineer and computational scientist working at the intersection of **quantitative systems pharmacology, mechanistic modeling, biomedical imaging, and machine learning**.

I earned my Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from the Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine and am currently a postdoctoral fellow at Johns Hopkins. My research focuses on using computational models to connect molecular mechanisms, patient variability, and treatment response.

My work has included mechanistic modeling of angiogenesis and hypoxia signaling, analysis of cerebral microcirculation, deep-learning prediction of blood-flow dynamics, and the development of virtual patient populations for evaluating cancer therapies.

I am particularly interested in expanding quantitative pharmacology approaches to **natural products and herbal medicines**. My long-term goal is to use mechanistic modeling, PK/PD, and QSP to better understand their biological effects, optimize combinations and dosing strategies, and support the rigorous development of accessible therapies.

## 📌 Selected Research

### 🧬 HIF Signaling Under Hypoxia

Developed and validated a mechanistic model of HIF1α- and HIF2α-mediated signaling in endothelial cells, including oxygen-dependent responses and VEGF regulation.

[📄 Read the publication](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.isci.2026.116195)

### 🩸 HGF–VEGF Signaling in Endothelial Cells

Developed a data-driven mechanistic model investigating how HGF and VEGF regulate endothelial proliferation, permeability, and angiogenic signaling.

[📄 Read the publication](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.isci.2025.113199)

### 🔬 Endothelial Patterning Under Hypoxia

Developed a mechanistic model of Notch–Dll4 signaling to investigate endothelial tip–stalk cell decisions and vascular patterning under hypoxic conditions.

[📄 Read the publication](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fphys.2024.1351753/full)

### 🧪 Quantitative Systems Pharmacology in Oncology

Developing mechanistic QSP models and virtual patient populations to evaluate cancer therapies, treatment combinations, biomarkers of response, and interpatient variability.

### 🧠 Blood-Flow Prediction from Longitudinal Imaging

Developed deep-learning approaches, including recurrent and attention-based models, to predict cortical blood-flow dynamics from longitudinal microvascular imaging.

### 🖼️ Biomedical Image-Analysis Pipelines

Built computational workflows for segmentation, feature extraction, and quantitative analysis of microCT, light-sheet, laser speckle, and intrinsic optical signal images.
